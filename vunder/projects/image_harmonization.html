Image Harmonization


#image_harmonization

#image_manipulation

Image harmonization is an important step in photo editing to achieve visual consistency in composite images by adjusting the appearances of the foreground to make it compatible with the background. Previous harmonization methods imply training encoder-decoder networks from scratch. We claim this approach is overcomplicated since learning a high-level representation of objects is a challenging task.

Instead, we proposed a novel architecture to utilize the space of high-level features learned by a pre-trained classification network. We create our models using existing encoder-decoder architectures and a pre-trained foreground-aware deep high-resolution network. We extensively evaluate the proposed method on the existing image harmonization benchmark and set up a new state-of-the-art.
